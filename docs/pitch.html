Why now

Everyone crowds into US equities. The ground is shifting.

~60%

USD share of global FX reserves today — down from ~70% two decades ago.

Over-concentration in a single market is itself a risk. Global capital is looking to diversify — and the next decade's growth engine increasingly runs through Asia.

Where the growth is

The hottest space in the world runs through Asia's supply chain.

Surging demand for GPUs, memory, and semiconductors is the defining trade of the decade. Follow that supply chain and it runs deep through Asia — and Japan is a critical link.

Equipment, test, and materials the whole industry depends on — globally important companies, listed in Tokyo.

The opportunity

The world's 3rd-largest stock market — and under-owned by global investors.

A good market to invest in. Yet surprisingly few international investors allocate meaningful capital to Japanese equities.

Why the gap?

Why it's hard

The data is fragmented — and in another language.

JPX· EDINET filings· Company disclosures· Data providers· Japanese-language sources

In the US, Fidelity and Macrotrends make this trivial — anyone can pull the critical info in seconds. There's no standout equivalent for Japan.

Investors spend more time gathering information than analyzing it.

The question

Can a system turn raw Japanese disclosures into an analyst-style first read…

…without fabricating the numbers?

That constraint — verifiable, traceable output — drove every design decision.

What I built — the prototype

EDINET filing → traceable, English research memo.

EDINET filing→ XBRL extraction→ Financials · history · peers · management→ Research memo

Core principle — structured data over LLM extraction. Every figure is pulled deterministically from XBRL, never guessed. LLMs only summarize and translate.

revenue = jppfs_cor:NetSales @ CurrentYearDuration  — every number traces back to the exact filing element.

The prototype in action

Type a ticker → a research memo.

Live walkthrough of the web UI — offline sample, no keys required.

How I know it works

It ships with its own benchmark.

  • Cross-source consistency — each figure must agree across two independent places in the XBRL.
  • Cash-flow reconciliation — Δcash ≈ operating + investing + financing.
  • LLM-as-judge — does the memo explain the earnings change, flag real risks, stay faithful to the figures?

Verified live across 5 semiconductor names: 100% extraction coverage, 100% cross-source consistency. Running it is what surfaced real-world edge cases (an IFRS filer, a 3-for-1 split) — caught and fixed.
